Rather than simply taking photographs, I create portraits that capture personality, presence, and character through a distinctive photographic process.

The Process

The mosaic panorama technique

The true distinction of my work lies in the way each portrait is created. Rather than relying on a single exposure, I carefully capture and combine multiple photographs into one seamless mosaic panorama.

The result is a portrait that combines the character of vintage optics with the precision of modern digital techniques. This approach produces images with extraordinary detail, remarkable depth, and an immersive, almost three-dimensional perspective.

The soul of it all, a 1957 Carl Zeiss Jena Biotar.

Every photographer develops their own visual signature. Mine is defined in part by my choice of lens – a beautiful piece of imperfection, produced in 1957. Contemporary lenses are engineered for technical perfection; mine brings character, subtle imperfections, and a distinctive rendering that gives each image a unique visual identity.
